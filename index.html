Pencils for Sketching

Graphite Pencils

A thin graphite core is embedded in a shell of other material. The pencil shell is typically wooden, but can be made of plastic or recycled paper.These are the most common pencils used in art and writing.

Charcoal Pencils

Create dark and rough lines, but not as versatile as the other options. Charcoal can produce lines that are very light or intensely black. The dry medium can be applied to almost any surface from smooth to very coarse.

B-series Pencils

The "B" gradings are on the soft and black side. B pencils are softer (more graphite, less clay) and tend to get blunt very quickly.

H-series Pencils

The "H" gradings are on the hard and light side. H pencils have a hard lead (more clay, less graphite) and remain sharp for a long time. These are not usually the best pencils for drawing and sketching.

HB Pencils

"HB" is your standard writing pencil. HB pencils sit midway and are a nice average pencil to use. This is usually your common office or school pencil. The best pencils for drawing and sketching are a HB

F-series Pencils

"F" is in-between 'H" and "B". F pencils have extremely hard lead and are only occasionally found in pencil sets.
